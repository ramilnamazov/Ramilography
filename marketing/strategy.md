## The brand you already have (and it's good)

Your site leads with **"Sculpting with light. Defining with shadow."** — a
premium, cinematic, fine-art positioning. We **keep that as the umbrella brand.**
It's a real differentiator and justifies higher prices. Don't water it down.

## The core tension to solve

A single moody "fine-art" homepage is great for *vibe* but bad for *getting
found and booked* across four different services. Someone Googling
"New Jersey real estate photographer" or "family photographer near me" won't
land on, or trust, an abstract art page.

**The fix: premium umbrella brand + dedicated service pages underneath.**

```
Ramilography (the art brand, cinematic feel)
├── /portraits   → families, individuals, maternity, headshots
├── /branding    → personal brand, product, food/restaurant content
├── /real-estate → listings, interiors, Airbnb/short-term rental
└── /portfolio   → the showcase (what you have now)
```

Each service page = its own headline, its own example gallery, its own
"who it's for," its own price-from, its own inquiry CTA. This is what makes
local SEO and (later) ads actually convert, while the homepage keeps the
high-end art feel.

## Positioning per niche (NJ/NYC angle)

- **Portraits/Family** — "Editorial-quality family & portrait photography in
  New Jersey." Sells emotion + heirloom quality. Highest volume, repeat yearly.
- **Brand/Product/Food** — "Content that makes NJ & NYC brands look expensive."
  Sells ROI to business clients. Highest $/repeat, B2B, recurring retainers.
- **Real estate/Spaces** — "Listings that sell faster. Interiors that book."
  Sells speed + bookings to agents/hosts. Recurring B2B, fast turnaround.

## How "all goals" gets sequenced (so it's not overwhelming)

1. **Bookings now** → service pages + Google Business Profile + fast inquiry replies.
2. **Audience** → content engine posting your best work consistently.
3. **Email list** → a simple "get my rate guide / book a mini-session" capture on the site.
4. **Products** → introduced later (prints, gift cards, mini-session packages) once traffic exists.

## Money order of operations
Free/organic foundation → prove inquiries convert → THEN scale with paid ads.
You don't spend a dollar on ads until the site turns visitors into inquiries
and we can measure it.
