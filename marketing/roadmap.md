# Roadmap — what we build, in what order

The order matters. Most photographers waste money running ads before the
website can convert and before tracking exists. We do it right: get the
foundation converting, then pour fuel (ads) on a fire that's already lit.

---

## Phase 0 — Foundation (Week 1) · mostly you + me, ~free
**Goal: know who we're talking to and have the assets to talk.**
- You fill in `00-intake-brand-brief.md` and drop photos in `assets/`.
- I write: positioning statement, ideal-client profile, offer/pricing review,
  3-word brand voice, and a content pillar plan.
- I claim/optimize the **Google Business Profile** (huge for local + free).
- Deliverable: a 1-page brand & strategy doc you approve.

## Phase 1 — Convert (Weeks 1–2) · me building, your approval
**Goal: when someone lands on you, they inquire.**
- Website audit + fixes: clear headline, portfolio, packages, an obvious
  "Book/Inquire" button, fast mobile load, an inquiry form that emails you.
- Install tracking: GA4, Search Console, **Meta Pixel** (so future ads work).
- Set up a fast inquiry-response template + auto-reply so no lead goes cold.
- Local SEO basics so you show up for "[your city] + [your photography type]".
- Deliverable: a site that turns visitors into inquiries, and we can measure it.

## Phase 2 — Content engine (Weeks 2–4) · the habit that compounds
**Goal: consistent, on-brand posting without it eating your week.**
- Content pillars (e.g. recent work / behind-the-scenes / client love / tips /
  offers) so we never stare at a blank page.
- A monthly **content calendar** in `content/` — I draft captions + hashtags +
  which photo, you approve in a batch, we schedule.
- Repurpose: 1 shoot → multiple posts, Reels, a Google post, a website update.
- Set up **automated scheduling** (start with free Meta Business Suite).
- Deliverable: 4 weeks of scheduled content, refreshed monthly.

## Phase 3 — Paid ads (Week 4+) · only when 0–2 are solid
**Goal: predictable, profitable lead flow.**
- Start small (your tested budget). Begin with the easiest wins:
  - **Retargeting**: cheap ads to people who visited your site / IG.
  - **Local awareness + lead ads** to your ideal-client audience.
- Strong creative (your best photos + a clear offer) beats clever targeting.
- We measure cost-per-inquiry and cost-per-booking, kill losers, scale winners.
- Deliverable: a small always-on ad system with reported ROI.

## Phase 4 — Follow-up & retention (ongoing) · the cheapest growth
**Goal: turn one client into reviews, referrals, and repeat work.**
- Post-shoot flow: deliver → ask for a Google review → ask for a referral.
- Light email list: seasonal mini-sessions, last-minute openings, holiday offers.
- Reactivate past clients (family photos every year, etc.).

## Phase 5 — Measure & iterate (monthly) · so we spend where it pays
- A simple monthly scorecard: inquiries, bookings, revenue, ad spend, what to
  do more/less of. No vanity metrics.

---

## Roughly what "running it" looks like each week
- **You:** approve a batch of content, reply to inquiries fast, shoot, send me
  new photos. ~1–2 focused hours.
- **Me:** produce content, manage scheduling, run/optimize ads, update the
  site, prep the monthly report, flag decisions that need you.

## What I can start doing the moment you say go
- Optimize the Google Business Profile (once I'm a manager).
- Write your positioning + first month of content from your brief + photos.
- Design templates and post graphics (I can produce these directly in Canva).
- Audit your current website and hand you a prioritized fix list.
